More than 586 species of resident and migratory birds have been sighted, including the crested serpent eagle, blossom-headed parakeet and the red junglefowl, owls and nightjars.[6] 33 species of reptiles including the Indian python,[6] seven species of amphibians, seven species of fish and 36 species of dragonflies have also been recorded.[8]

Mammals are represented by the Indian elephant, Indian leopard, Bengal tiger, jungle cat, fishing cat and leopard cat.[2][6] barking deer, sambar deer, hog deer and chital, sloth bear and Asiatic black bear, Indian gray mongoose, otters, yellow-throated marten, Himalayan goral, Indian pangolin, langur and rhesus macaque.[16] Marsh crocodiles and gharials were released into Ramganga river.[6] Thick jungle, the Ramganga river and plentiful prey make this reserve an ideal habitat for predators.[16] The tigers in the park kill large animals like water buffalo and even sub apex-sized elephants when other prey is scarce.[6][17]
